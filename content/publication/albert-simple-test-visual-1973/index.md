title: A Simple Test of Visual Neglect
authors:
- Martin L. Albert
date: '1973-01-01'
publishDate: '2023-11-03T03:35:12.103344Z'
publication_types:
- article-journal
publication: '*Neurology*'
doi: 10.1212/wnl.23.6.658
abstract: "A simple, clinically useful test for visual neglect was administered to
  96 subjects: 66 had unilateral, circumscribed, surgically verified, cerebral hemispheric
  lesions (36 LBD and 30 RBD); thirty were non-brain-damaged controls. Forty 2.5 cm
  lines were drawn in the presence of the subject in an apparently random manner on
  an unlined sheet of paper placed before the subject. The actual disposition of these
  lines was standardized, allowing for a systematic analysis of subjects' performance
  on the left, the right, and the center of the page. The subject was instructed to
  draw a line through all the lines. Scoring was based on the number and location
  of lines left uncrossed. Major results were as follows: (1) Visual neglect occurred
  in 30 percent of subjects with LBD and in 37 percent of those with RBD. (2) The
  severity of the neglect, but not the frequency, was significantly greater after
  RBD than LBD. (3) Visual neglect occurred in the center of the page or on both sides
  of the page more often than on only the side contralateral to the lesion. Visual
  neglect thus appears to be more a bilateral than a unilateral phenomenon. (4) Visual
  neglect may occur without a visual field defect, and a visual field defect may occur
  without visual neglect. This study supports the theoretical position that visual
  neglect is not a single process but rather is a manifestation of several different
  types of neurologic dysfunction. © 1973 American Academy of Neurology."
