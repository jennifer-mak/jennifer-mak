abstract: Stroke instigates regenerative responses that reorganize connectivity patterns
  among surviving neurons. The new connectivity patterns can be suboptimal for behavioral
  function. This review summarizes current knowledge on post-stroke motor system reorganization
  and emerging strategies for shaping it with manipulations of behavior and cortical
  activity to improve functional outcome.
