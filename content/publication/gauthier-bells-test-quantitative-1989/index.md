abstract: 'The Bells Test, a cancellation task, permits a quantitative and qualitative
  evaluation of visual neglect. The construction of the test allows for rapid visualization
  of the spatial distribution of the omitted targets and their quantification. The
  examiner can also obtain a qualitative picture through an approximation of the visual
  scanning pattern used by the subject: this provides valuable information on \"how\"
  the task is performed. In summary, the Bells Test is a more dynamic, and thus, more
  sensitive clinical examination for visual neglect.'
