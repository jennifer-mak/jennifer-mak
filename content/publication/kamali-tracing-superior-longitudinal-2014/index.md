abstract: The major language pathways such as superior longitudinal fasciculus (SLF)
  pathways have been outlined by experimental and diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) studies.
  The SLF I and some of the superior parietal lobule connections of the SLF pathways
  have not been depicted by prior DTI studies due to the lack of imaging sensitivity
  and adequate spatial resolution. In the current study, the trajectory of the SLF
  fibers has been delineated on five healthy human subjects using diffusion tensor
  tractography on a 3.0-T scanner at high spatial resolution. We also demonstrate
  for the first time the trajectory and connectivity of the SLF fibers in relation
  to other language pathways as well as the superior parietal lobule connections of
  the language circuit using high spatial resolution DTI in the healthy adult human
  brain. © 2012 Springer-Verlag Berlin Heidelberg.
