abstract: We used the paired associative stimulation (PAS) technique to investigate
  associative plasticity of the sensorimotor cortex in 16 Parkinson's disease (PD)
  patients off and on therapy and in 10 age-matched controls. After PAS, motor evoked
  potential (MEP) amplitudes increased more and the cortical silent period showed
  a reduced prolongation in patients off therapy than in controls. These changes lasted
  for at least 30 minutes. In addition, MEP amplitudes increased in a less focal manner
  in patients off therapy than in controls. After patients received dopaminergic therapy,
  these abnormalities normalized. The abnormal responsiveness of sensorimotor cortex
  neurons to PAS in PD patients off therapy probably reflects disordered plasticity
  within the motor cortex.
