publication: '*Case Reports in Clinical Medicine*'
doi: 10.4236/crcm.2018.710045
abstract: Unilateral spatial neglect (USN) is characterized by neglect in peripersonal
  and extrapersonal space, the disparity of which is unknown. We assessed these forms
  of neglect in a patient with USN. We promoted sight scanning in extrapersonal space
  and used an eye camera to measure gaze movement. We found left-sided sight scanning
  in peripersonal space, but right-side-biased sight scanning in extrapersonal space.
  Additionally, line of sight was corrected when the patient was instructed to look
  left in extrapersonal space and to focus the line of sight at the center. Gaze measurement
  thus helped to assess disparities in spatial neglect in USN.
