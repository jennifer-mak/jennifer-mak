abstract: Patients with left-sided visuospatial neglect, typically after damage to
  the right parietal lobe, show a systematic bias towards larger numbers when asked
  to bisect a numerical interval. This has been taken as further evidence for a spatial
  representation of numbers, perhaps akin to a mental number line with smaller numbers
  represented to the left and larger numbers to the right. Previously, contralateral
  neglect-like symptoms in physical line bisection have been induced in healthy subjects
  with repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation (rTMS) over right posterior parietal
  lobe. Here we used rTMS over parietal and occipital sites in healthy subjects to
  investigate spatial representations in a number bisection task. Subjects were asked
  to name the midpoint of numerical intervals without calculating. On control trials
  subjects' behaviour was similar to performance reported in physical line bisection
  experiments. Subjects underestimated the midpoint of the numerical interval. Repetitive
  transcranial magnetic stimulation produced representational neglect-like symptoms
  in number bisection when applied over right posterior parietal cortex (right PPC).
  Repetitive TMS over right PPC shifted the perceived midpoint of the numerical interval
  significantly to the right while occipital TMS had no effect on bisection performance.
  Our study therefore provides further evidence that subjects use spatial representations,
  perhaps akin to a mental number line, in basic numerical processing tasks. Furthermore,
  we showed that the right posterior parietal cortex is crucially involved in spatial
  representation of numbers. © 2005 Elsevier Ltd. All rights reserved.
