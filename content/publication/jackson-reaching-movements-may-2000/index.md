title: Reaching Movements May Reveal the Distorted Topography of Spatial Representations
  after Neglect
authors:
- Stephen R. Jackson
- Roger Newport
- Masud Husain
- Monika Harvey
- John V. Hindle
date: '2000-01-01'

publication: '*Neuropsychologia*'
doi: 10.1016/S0028-3932(99)00083-4
abstract: It has been proposed that patients with spatial neglect fail to respond
  appropriately toward stimuli opposite their brain lesion because they have an impairment
  of directing attention. However, a disorder of 'intention' - or movement initiation
  - has also been demonstrated in this condition. Recently, the paths of neglect patients'
  reaches have been shown to be abnormally curved, but it is unclear whether this
  impairment is visual or motor. Here, we show for the first time that reaches to
  and from identical positions executed by three patients recovering from neglect
  are significantly more curved to visually defined targets compared to when the same
  targets are defined proprioceptively. These findings indicate that abnormal hand
  paths in neglect result from an impairment in the visual representation of space
  used to guide reaches but without any general failure of spatial representation
  of target position. Furthermore, the curved hand paths reveal how the topography
  of that representation is distorted in spatial neglect. Copyright (C) 2000 Elsevier
  Science Ltd.
