abstract: Optogenetics, a technique that utilizes light-sensitive ion channels or
  pumps to activate or inhibit neurons, has allowed scientists unprecedented precision
  and control for manipulating neuronal activity. With the clinical need to develop
  more precise and effective therapies for patients with drug-resistant epilepsy,
  these tools have recently been explored as a novel treatment for halting seizure
  activity in various animal models. In this review, we provide a detailed and current
  summary of these optogenetic approaches and provide a perspective on their future
  clinical application as a potential neuromodulatory therapy.
