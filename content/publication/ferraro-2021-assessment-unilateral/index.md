abstract: Among the several disabling features following a stroke, the unilateral
  spatial neglect (USN) is one of the most frequently present in case of right-sided
  lesions. The standard assessment of such a pathology happens via tests that are
  usually performed on paper. However, a great interest toward digital solutions for
  USN is arising, in order to facilitate the activities of medical specialists. Furthermore,
  the recent outbreak of COVID-19 is stressing the importance of remote assistive
  tools which enable the continuity of care at patient's home. Indeed, connected technology
  allows for remote monitoring of patients by the clinical staff, hence enabling the
  definition of a personalized plan of care even after the de-hospitalization and
  in safe conditions. In addition, USN patients are among the so-called fragile subjects
  who, due to their condition, require continuous monitoring and rehabilitative therapeutic
  intervention, which can be granted by some ICT solution. The present work introduces
  the battery of tests for the assessment of USN which are included in the ReMoVES
  system. They are digital versions of standard tests that can be practiced via a
  touchscreen. The informative content that such activities deliver is wider and more
  comprehensive than the paper based versions. The experimental phase is described
  with respect to the well-known Albert's test, in a real-life scenario. The indicators
  that can be computed are here defined and described.
